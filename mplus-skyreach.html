Skyreach

4 Bosses • Spires of Arak • The arakkoa's sky fortress. Features pillar-based Four Winds avoidance, Solar Construct add management, and Lens Flare interrupt checks on the final boss — one of the highest interrupt-demand finales in the pool.

Tactyks Guide — Skyreach

⚔ Trash Pull Notes

📶 Priority Targets
  • Arakkoa Windcaller — Wind Shear silences the entire party. Kill or interrupt before anything else; a silenced healer ends the pull.
  • Arakkoa Dustscribe — Arcane Bolt Volley hits all players. Kill immediately after the Windcaller or in parallel.
  • Solar Familiar — small construct that empowers the nearest Windcaller. Kill the Familiar before the Windcaller when both are present.
  • Skyreach Talon Guard — charge-stuns the tank and switches targets. Tank re-establishes immediately; DPS stun to cancel the charge if possible.
⚡ Must Kick
  • Wind Shear (Arakkoa Windcaller) — silences all players simultaneously. This is the dungeon's most dangerous trash ability on high keys.
  • Arcane Bolt (Arakkoa Dustscribe) — fast-cast high-damage arcane bolt. Rotate all 3 DPS kicks.
  • Call of the Sun (Arakkoa High Sage) — empowers all nearby arakkoa. Interrupt before the buff applies to the pack.
  • Solar Blast (Skyreach Solarflare) — ignites the floor under players. Interrupt to prevent persistent fire patches.
🔒 CC Targets
  • Arakkoa Windcallers — Polymorph (humanoid). Highest CC priority. CC before the pull when facing two Windcallers.
  • Arakkoa Dustscribes — Polymorph, Hex. CC when pulling packs that contain both a Windcaller and a Dustscribe.
  • Arakkoa Bladetalons — Polymorph, Hex, Imprison (DH). CC to prevent back-to-back charge-stuns on the tank.
  • Skyreach Talon Guards — Imprison, Ring of Frost (Mage), Freezing Trap (Hunter).
🔮 Purge / Soothe
  • Call of the Sun buff on arakkoa allies — Spellsteal (Mage) is strongest here (steal the damage amp). Purge (Shaman), Devour Magic (Warlock), or Naturalize (Evoker) also work.
  • Solar Enrage (any arakkoa empowered by Solar Familiar) — Soothe (Druid) or Naturalize (Evoker) removes the enrage before the tank spikes.
Boss 1 of 4

Ranjit

Not a standard tank fight. Ranjit flies and periodically lands. Wind Chakrams rotate and return — never stand in their path. Fan of Blades applies a group bleed. Know your safe zone before the pull.

🛡 Tank
  • When Ranjit lands, reposition him away from returning chakram paths to protect melee DPS.
  • During aerial phases, stay behind a safe pillar — Wind Chakrams clear pillar zones.
  • Piercing Rush is a charge attack — position to absorb the impact without it clipping the group.
✚ Healer
  • Fan of Blades applies a bleed to the whole group — keep rolling HoTs up at all times. Use cooldowns when multiple players have stacking bleed.
  • Aerial phases have reduced tank damage — use these windows to top the group.
  • Do not stand in Wind Chakram paths to cast — find a safe position and maintain uptime from there.
⚔ DPS
  • Track Wind Chakram paths — they rotate and return. Never be in their path. Assign safe zones before the pull.
  • Burn hard during Ranjit's grounded phases — he takes significantly reduced damage while airborne.
  • Destroy Disruption Crystals when they spawn — they grant Ranjit a damage-absorbing shield.
Boss 2 of 4

Araknath

Solar Infusion heals Araknath from Solar Beams unless players stand in them to block (soak). Everyone rotates soak duty. Supernova is the primary group damage spike — top everyone before it fires.

🛡 Tank
  • Face Araknath toward an empty wall area — Solarflare pools persist and fill the room. Manage available floor space.
  • When Solar Constructs spawn, grab aggro on them to prevent them from pathing to Araknath and healing him.
  • Fire vulnerability stacks up on you — track it and communicate to the healer when you are at high stacks.
✚ Healer
  • Players soaking Solar Beams take Solar Flame fire damage — heal soak participants aggressively during each beam.
  • Top everyone off before Supernova fires — it is a high-damage group explosion and fire vulnerability debuffs amplify it.
  • Dispel Primal Fury enrage if it appears — it dramatically increases damage output.
⚔ DPS
  • Everyone rotates Solar Beam soaking — standing in a beam blocks Solar Infusion from healing Araknath. Set a soak order before the pull.
  • Kick: Haywire every cast — uninterrupted, it deals platform-wide fire AoE and applies an enrage buff. Soothe: Druid (Soothe) or Evoker (Naturalize) must remove the enrage immediately if Haywire lands.
  • Kill Solar Constructs before they reach Araknath — each one that connects heals him significantly.
Boss 3 of 4

Rukhran

Stay in melee range of Rukhran at all times — his proximity suppresses Screech entirely. If anyone drifts out of melee, he casts it. Pierce Armor reduces the tank's armor — use mitigation immediately after.

🛡 Tank
  • Stay in permanent melee contact with Rukhran — proximity suppresses his Screech cast entirely. If you move away, Screech fires on the whole group.
  • Pierce Armor reduces your armor value — use active mitigation and cooldowns immediately after it is applied.
  • Position Rukhran at the platform edge — Dawnwings adds spawn and are easier to collect at edges.
✚ Healer
  • Searing Quills is a burst AoE on the whole group — use a short cooldown for every cast. They come frequently.
  • Dawnwings adds deal consistent AoE damage while alive — keep group health up during add waves.
  • On Mythic+: Breaking Dawn resurrects killed adds. Prioritize interrupting this cast or adds respawn mid-fight.
⚔ DPS
  • Stay in melee range of Rukhran at all times to suppress Screech. If you need to move, communicate so someone else can maintain melee contact.
  • Kill Dawnwings adds immediately upon spawn — they fixate and deal dangerous damage if left alive.
  • Kick: On Mythic+, interrupt Breaking Dawn resurrection cast — failing this respawns dead adds. Also interrupt Sacred Blade Volley when it casts.
Boss 4 of 4 — Final

High Sage Viryx

Solar Zealots grab players and carry them toward the platform edge — carried players die if dropped off. Kill Zealots immediately, always. Interrupt Cast Down to limit how many Zealots spawn.

🛡 Tank
  • Lens Flare is a targeted beam — on Mythic+ it leaves Blazing Ground patches that permanently shrink safe floor space. Plan your movement to minimize patch coverage.
  • Solar Blast is the primary tank damage ability — use defensive cooldowns.
  • Watch for Solar Zealots grabbing players — if a healer is grabbed, call it immediately.
✚ Healer
  • If a Solar Zealot grabs you, call it immediately — you are being carried toward the edge and will die if DPS does not kill the Zealot in time.
  • Heal players being carried by Zealots — they take damage during the carry.
  • On Mythic+, Blazing Ground patches from Lens Flare accumulate and shrink the platform — plan healing movement accordingly.
⚔ DPS
  • Kill Solar Zealots the instant they grab a player — always. A completed carry means a dead teammate.
  • Kick: Cast Down — interrupt it aggressively to control Zealot count. Fewer spawns means fewer simultaneous carries to manage.
  • Set the interrupt rotation (DPS 1 → DPS 2 → DPS 3) before the pull — Lens Flare has a fast cast time with zero forgiveness on high keys.
