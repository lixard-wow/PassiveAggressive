Maisara Caverns

3 Bosses • Zul'Aman • Vilebranch trolls sacrifice captives to power necromantic rituals binding Rak'tul in the depths. The most mechanically complex and interrupt-heavy dungeon of Season 1.

Tactyks Guide — Maisara Caverns

⚔ Trash Pull Notes

📶 Priority Targets
  • Vilebranch Shadowcaster — backline caster nuking the whole party. Kill before anything else.
  • Necrotic Channeler — empowers all nearby undead by 50% damage. Kill or interrupt on sight.
  • Rak'tul's Bound Shades — fixate on the healer if not taunted. Tank picks them up immediately; DPS burns them fast.
  • Vilebranch Witch Doctor — self-heals and heals nearby allies. Kill before healers become unkillable.
⚡ Must Kick
  • Shadowbolt Volley (Shadowcaster) — party-wide shadow blast. Top interrupt priority.
  • Necrotic Surge (Necrotic Channeler) — empowers all nearby mobs; a missed kick snowballs immediately.
  • Troll Regeneration (Witch Doctor) — heals the Witch Doctor and nearby allies for large amounts.
  • Hex (Vilebranch Hexer) — CC's a random party member. Interrupt to prevent losing a DPS mid-pull.
🔒 CC Targets
  • Vilebranch Hexers — Polymorph, Hex, Imprison (humanoid). CC before the pull if pulling multiple.
  • Vilebranch Shadowcasters — Polymorph or Sheep them while burning the Witch Doctor first.
  • Necrotic Channelers — CC if you can't kill them immediately; note they break on AoE damage.
🔮 Purge / Soothe
  • Berserking (Vilebranch Warriors) — Soothe (Druid) or Naturalize (Evoker) removes this enrage.
  • Spirit Shield (Witch Doctor) — Purge (Shaman), Spellsteal (Mage), or Devour Magic (Warlock) strips the absorb shield.
Boss 1 of 3

Muro'jin & Nekraxx

Muro'jin the hunter and Nekraxx his dire bat. Kill Muro'jin first. If he dies and Revive Pet goes uninterrupted, Nekraxx returns at 35% HP. If Nekraxx dies first, she enrages and ramps damage fast.

🛡 Tank
  • Focus both bosses but let Muro'jin die first. His death triggers Revive Pet — interrupt it immediately. If Nekraxx dies first instead, she gains Bestial Wrath: 20% more damage every 4 seconds.
  • Muro'jin drops hunting traps around the arena. A player intentionally stepping on a trap freezes them briefly — Nekraxx's Carrion Swoop will strike the frozen player and stun her for 5 seconds instead of knocking someone into the air. Use this proactively.
  • Rotate defensives for each boss — they deal different damage types so the pressure alternates.
✚ Healer
  • When Muro'jin dies, be ready for a burst window — Nekraxx accelerates rapidly if Revive Pet is not interrupted.
  • Infected Pinions applies a plague to all players — use major cooldowns during this, not after.
  • Dispel bleed and nature effects promptly. Troll bosses apply persistent DoTs that compound at high stack counts.
⚔ DPS
  • Kick: Revive Pet (Muro'jin) — highest priority interrupt in this fight. If it lands, Nekraxx returns at 35% HP mid-fight. Rotate all 3 DPS on it.
  • Focus Muro'jin first. Maintain enough pressure on Nekraxx to prevent her from running rampant, but kill Muro'jin before finishing her.
  • Use the trap mechanic intentionally: step on Muro'jin's traps to freeze yourself and bait Nekraxx's Carrion Swoop charge into hitting you, stunning her for 5 seconds.
Boss 2 of 3

Vordaza

Phantom collision mechanics define this fight. Unstable Phantoms must be killed, but the Soulrot pools they leave persist. Necrotic Convergence is only interruptible after the Deathshroud shield is destroyed first.

🛡 Tank
  • Drain Soul is a significant tank buster with a healing reduction debuff — use major defensives and call it out so the healer can compensate.
  • Vordaza resets aggro at Phase 2 entry — taunt immediately to prevent healer threat pulls.
  • Keep Vordaza positioned to give DPS room to chase Unstable Phantoms without moving through Soulrot pools.
✚ Healer
  • Soulrot pools left by dead Unstable Phantoms persist — keep the group moving and plan safe healing positions.
  • Drain Soul on the tank applies a healing reduction — use stronger direct heals to compensate. Have a cooldown ready.
  • Coordinate phantom elimination with DPS — each dead phantom leaves a ground hazard, so cluster kills in one area when possible.
⚔ DPS
  • Kill Unstable Phantoms quickly — leave Soulrot pools in one corner of the arena to preserve safe floor space.
  • Destroy the Deathshroud shield before Necrotic Convergence channels — the channel cannot be interrupted until the shield is broken.
  • Kick: Necrotic Convergence — only possible after the shield is down. Assign one DPS to break shield, then interrupt the channel immediately.
Boss 3 of 3 — Final

Rak'tul, Vessel of Souls

The dungeon's final boss. Soulbind Totems drain your teammates. Soulrending Roar sends players into a spirit realm with escalating damage. Spiritbreaker leaves patches that increase damage taken.

🛡 Tank
  • Avoid being launched into Spectral Decay patches (left by Spiritbreaker) — anyone standing in them takes 30% increased damage from enemies.
  • Position Rak'tul away from existing Spectral Decay patches to preserve safe tanking positions.
  • Rak'tul ruptures every 6 seconds in engorged form, dealing ~55,000 Shadow damage to the group — use a defensive and call it out so the healer can pre-cast.
✚ Healer
  • Players sent into the spirit realm take escalating Withering Soul damage — coordinate their return timing to minimize time spent inside.
  • Prepare for the recurring rupture pulses from Rak'tul's engorged form — use cooldowns before each pulse, not after.
  • Soulbind Totems drain teammates passively — DPS must destroy them immediately; the damage adds up fast while they are alive.
⚔ DPS
  • Destroy Soulbind Totems the instant they spawn — they drain all teammates and empower the boss while active.
  • Manage soul realm transitions — minimize the number of players inside simultaneously to reduce Withering Soul overlap.
  • Dodge Volatile Essence explosion zones and move out of Spectral Decay patches immediately on contact.
