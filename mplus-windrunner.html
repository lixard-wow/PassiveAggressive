Windrunner Spire

4 Bosses • Eversong Woods • Once the proud estate of the Windrunner family, now haunted by banshees, fire creatures, and spectral guardians loyal to ghosts of the past.

Tactyks Guide — Windrunner Spire

⚔ Trash Pull Notes

📶 Priority Targets
  • Keening Banshee — her Wail of Despair silences the entire party. Kill immediately or the pull becomes unmanageable.
  • Spectral Warden — buffs all nearby undead with Unholy Fortitude (+40% damage). Die or purge this before other mobs are addressed.
  • Ghostly Archivist — casts shadow bolts that chain between players. Kill before they stack chain damage.
  • Flamestep Hatchlings — low HP packs near Emberdawn's area. AoE them down before burn stacks compound on the tank.
⚡ Must Kick
  • Wail of Despair (Keening Banshee) — silences all players simultaneously, preventing all casts including heals. Highest interrupt priority in the dungeon.
  • Unholy Bolt (Spectral Warden) — stacking shadow damage on a random player; stops the chain before it reaches lethal stacks.
  • Flame Burst (fire trash near Emberdawn) — AoE fire damage hitting all nearby players.
  • Spirit Drain (Ghostly Archivist) — channels damage while healing itself. Interrupt to stop both simultaneously.
🔒 CC Targets
  • Keening Banshees — Shackle Undead (Priest), Banish (Warlock/DH on spirit types). Do not Polymorph — they are undead.
  • Spectral Wardens — Shackle Undead (Priest). CC if you cannot kill them before Unholy Fortitude applies.
  • Ghostly Footmen — Shackle Undead (Priest), Imprison (Demon Hunter).
🔮 Purge / Soothe
  • Unholy Fortitude (Spectral Warden buff on allies) — Purge (Shaman), Spellsteal (Mage), Devour Magic (Warlock), Naturalize (Evoker). Stripping this removes the damage amp from the entire pack.
  • Spectral Rage (any enraged ghost) — Soothe (Druid) or Naturalize (Evoker) removes the enrage.
Boss 1 of 4

Emberdawn

A fire creature defending her nest. Flaming Updraft marks 2 players — detonate away from the group. Burning Gale intermission at 100 energy is the primary damage spike. Searing Beak is the tank buster.

🛡 Tank
  • Searing Beak is the primary tank buster — use a major defensive every time it is cast.
  • During Burning Gale intermission, position in a corner — fire breath patterns and rotating frontals are more manageable from a fixed corner position.
  • Face Emberdawn away from the group during normal phases — frontal fire attacks hit anyone in the arc.
✚ Healer
  • Use major cooldowns just before Burning Gale intermission begins — it is heavy sustained fire damage to the whole group.
  • Flaming Updraft players detonate away from you — pre-position to keep the group topped before the detonation.
  • Between intermissions, maintain steady throughput — Searing Beak spikes hit hard and come frequently.
⚔ DPS
  • Flaming Updraft marks 2 players with an explosion debuff — move away from the group immediately and detonate in an open area.
  • Save burst cooldowns for ground phases — reduced damage window during Burning Gale means your uptime on the boss drops.
  • Kick: Flame Shriek — it silences and disorients the entire party simultaneously. Rotate all 3 DPS.
Boss 2 of 4

Derelict Duo

Kalis the banshee and Latch the abomination. Both must die within seconds of each other or the survivor triggers Broken Bond (50% damage ramp, stacking). Use Latch's Heaving Yank to interrupt Kalis's Debilitating Shriek.

🛡 Tank
  • Tank both Kalis and Latch together. Keep their HP within 10% of each other at all times — Broken Bond enrages the survivor if one dies too far ahead of the other.
  • Latch's Heaving Yank pulls you to him — use this intentionally to interrupt Kalis's Debilitating Shriek channel if the timing lines up.
  • Latch cleaves — face him away from the group. Call the coordinated burn when both are below 20%.
✚ Healer
  • Debilitating Shriek from Kalis ramps group damage significantly if it completes channeling — interrupt it via Latch's Heaving Yank or a DPS kick.
  • Watch for Curse of Darkness on Mythic+ — it summons fixating shadow entities. Call it immediately and have DPS kill them.
  • Two simultaneous damage streams — prioritize whichever player is spiking hardest.
⚔ DPS
  • Cleave both bosses evenly — do not let one fall more than 10% ahead of the other or Broken Bond wipes the group.
  • Kick: Kalis's Shadow Bolt — rotate all 3 DPS. If Latch's Heaving Yank is active, it also interrupts Kalis's Debilitating Shriek channel.
  • Kill fixating shadow entities from Curse of Darkness (Mythic+) immediately — they cannot be ignored.
Boss 3 of 4

Commander Kroluk

Reckless Leap targets the farthest player twice in a row. Stack melee with one player slightly behind to bait the first leap — this ensures the second lands on the tank. At 66% and 33%, Rallying Bellow: boss is fully immune until all adds are dead.

🛡 Tank
  • Stay slightly behind the melee stack — Reckless Leap targets the farthest player. The second leap always lands on the same spot as the first, so the tank baiting the first leap should move after landing.
  • At 66% and 33% HP, Rallying Bellow activates: Kroluk gains Shield Wall (99% damage reduction) and summons adds. He is completely immune until all adds are dead.
  • Group the four Haunting Grunts together for AoE. Use a grip or slow on the Spectral Axethrower to keep it near the Phantasmal Mystic for cleave.
✚ Healer
  • Rallying Bellow add phases are spike damage moments — cooldowns when the add warparty spawns.
  • Intimidating Shout fears players who are not near an ally — call out if you are isolated and feared.
  • Save a short cooldown for Rampage channel — it is heavy tank damage and comes between add phases.
⚔ DPS
  • Add kill order during Rallying Bellow: Phantasmal Mystic first (interrupt Chain Lightning), Spectral Axethrower second, Haunting Grunts last with AoE.
  • Kick: Phantasmal Mystic's Chain Lightning — highest interrupt priority during every add phase. This is the wipe condition if missed.
  • Stay within melee range of at least one other player at all times — Intimidating Shout fears isolated players.
Boss 4 of 4 — Final

The Restless Heart

Squall Leap stacks a DoT on players over time. The only way to cleanse it is to step onto Arrow Rain circles on the ground. Bullseye Windblast stuns anyone not standing on a circle.

🛡 Tank
  • Tempest Slash knocks you back — pre-position near Arrow Rain circles so the knockback lands you in one, which cancels the effect.
  • Track Squall Leap stacks on yourself — communicate with DPS on which circles you are using so you do not compete for the same one.
  • Gust Shot fires AoE that clears puddles and overlaps circles — position to allow multiple circles to be cleared in the same sweep.
✚ Healer
  • Squall Leap stacks are the steady-state damage source — use burst cooldowns when multiple players have high stacks and circles are on cooldown.
  • Ensure every player knows to step on Arrow Rain circles to cleanse — uncleansed stacks compound quickly.
  • Do not cluster the group on the same circles — spread slightly so multiple players can cleanse simultaneously.
⚔ DPS
  • Step onto Arrow Rain circles to remove Squall Leap stacks — this is the core fight mechanic. Circles spawn regularly; know where they are at all times.
  • When Bullseye Windblast is cast, be standing on an Arrow Rain circle — players hit outside a circle are stunned and take heavy damage.
  • Maintain boss uptime between cleanses. Circles are plentiful enough to allow short windows of standing on one without fully dropping DPS.
